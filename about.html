About

Platonic cuddle therapy is at the intersection of therapeutic touch and emotional care. It doesn’t fall under regulated massage therapy or regulated mental health services, but has instead emerged as a distinct offering within the broader field of wellness, with numerous health benefits.
I’m Cuddly Kathy, here to provide you with the therapeutic benefits of platonic cuddling! Snuggling is a great way to brighten up your day. I’m a young, fit, active woman offering professional, affectionate cuddle sessions. My goal is to make you feel happy, comfortable, and accepted while you enjoy the healing powers of platonic closeness!

While cuddling, you can feel free to tell me anything confidentially. Many clients are struggling with a breakup, loss, health issues, or other concerns. I am supportive, caring, and most importantly judgment-free!

My credentials are as follows:
  • Bachelor of Arts in Psychology
  • Crisis Line work experience
  • Integrative Trauma Practitioner certification
  • Yoga Teacher Certification (YTT-200)
  • Professional Cuddler Certification

In today’s world, many of us are finding ourselves increasingly distanced from one of our most basic human needs: safe, nurturing touch. Shifts in how we use technology and changes in the ways we connect, date, and experience intimacy all play a role. Consent culture has brought important awareness and encouraged people to honor their boundaries and speak their truth, which is deeply meaningful. At the same time, it has also led to a growing hesitation around physical touch, with some people unsure of how to engage without sending the wrong message.

As our collective understanding of consent deepens, there’s a beautiful opportunity to become more intentional and skillful in how we communicate and relate. Cuddling offers a supportive space to explore those personal boundaries, strengthen communication skills, and nurture healthier relationships through clear, caring touch.

It’s also a space for co-regulation, as sharing presence with a calm, grounded person can help your nervous system soften and settle. Many people move through their days in a low-grade state of stress (fight, flight, freeze, or fawn) without even realizing it. Therapeutic touch can invite the body into a parasympathetic state, helping you feel safe, connected, and at ease. It can lower stress hormones, release oxytocin (the bonding hormone), and offer a deep sense of emotional well-being.
